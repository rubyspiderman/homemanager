What's This - Capital Improvement

Check this box if the expenditure can be considered a capital improvement. These costs can be deducted from your capital gains on a property at the time of sale and are important to record over time. This is especially important on a second home or an investment property.

A capital improvement increases the value of your home, while a non-eligible repair just returns something to its original condition. According to the IRS, capital improvements have to last for more than one year and add value to your home, prolong its life, or adapt it to new uses.

Valid capital improvement examples:

  • Adding a deck, pool or sunroom
  • Renovating the kitchen or bathroom
  • Installing a new furnace or hot water heater
  • Publication 523 has a list of eligible improvements (SEE PAGE 9)

Not valid items (Repairs and Routine Maintenance):

  • Painting the house
  • Cleaning carpets, drapes, or air ducts
  • Repairing a broken handrail in a shower or staircase (simply restores the original function of the property)
